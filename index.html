A few more Tips:
- Most computers when shut off use very minimal energy, thus one suggestion we have is to try to shut down your computer at the end of the day, to prevent the computer from running at full power all day long!
- Do not let your computer charge overnight, it causes power loss and inefficiency
- Think about your carbon footprint while using your computer so that you are aware of your actions and carbon consumption
- Powerful computers can take large amounts of power so pay attention to energy efficient devices



Supercomputer Consumption (Each dot represents a tree)

The MIT Satori supercomputer, located at MIT is a supercomputer donated by IBM. Of all the supercomputers, it only uses 94 killawats/hour. We see that this value is extremely low compared to that of other supercomputers. This means that the energy output is also extremely low. As we can see in the picture, the Satori computer uses about 9 trees worth of CO2 per year.

The Electra, located in California, has a rating of 1,070 kW. It was ranked 46th in supercomputer system speed in June. Relative to the other supercomputers, we see that it is moderate in regards to its energy usage only consuming about 1500 trees per year.

The Supercomputer Fugaku, found in Japan, was ranked as the fastest system in June 2020. It has a rating of 28,334.5 kW, which is almost three times the power usage of IBM’s Summit, the 2nd fastest system on the list. This leads to it using up about 2,764 trees worth of carbon dioxide, a huge amount!

Computer Your Personal Power Consumption

How much carbon is emitted from your personal setup?
Follow these easy steps to find out more:
1. Download this python file: Github Python Personal Computer Energy Consumption
2. Go to your linux terminal and cd to the location of the downloaded personalEnergy.py file.
3. Run the command: python personalEnergy.py
4. Your terminal should display the energy usage report. You can use this to gain further insights into your computer's specific energy usage and CO2 emissions.
